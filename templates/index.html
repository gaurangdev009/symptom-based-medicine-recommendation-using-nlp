Most Common Diseases

Infectious diseases: Chickenpox, common cold, diphtheria, E. coli, giardiasis, HIV/AIDS, infectious mononucleosis, influenza (flu)

Other common diseases: Allergies, conjunctivitis ("pink eye"), diarrhea

Chronic diseases: Heart disease, diabetes

Other common health issues: Overweight and obesity, tobacco, substance abuse, mental health, injury and violence, environmental quality

Causes Of Most common Diseases

Bacteria. These one-cell organisms are responsible for illnesses such as strep throat, urinary tract infections and tuberculosis.

Viruses. Even smaller than bacteria, viruses cause a multitude of diseases ranging from the common cold to AIDS.

Fungi. Many skin diseases, such as ringworm and athlete's foot, are caused by fungi. Other types of fungi can infect your lungs or nervous system.

Parasites. Malaria is caused by a tiny parasite that is transmitted by a mosquito bite. Other parasites may be transmitted to humans from animal feces.

Prevention

Follow these tips to decrease the risk of infection:

Wash your hands. This is especially important before and after preparing food, before eating, and after using the toilet.

Get vaccinated. Vaccination can drastically reduce your chances of contracting many diseases.

Stay home when ill. Don't go to work if you are vomiting, have diarrhea or have a fever. Don't send your child to school if he or she has these signs, either.

Don't share personal items. Use your own toothbrush, comb and razor. Avoid sharing drinking glasses or dining utensils.

Travel wisely. If you're traveling out of the country, talk to your doctor about any special vaccinations — such as yellow fever, cholera, hepatitis A or B, or typhoid fever — you may need.

The Epic Guide to Seasonal Eating

Summer

Summer is a common season for thunderstorms. When the earth tilts towards the Sun, the more heat we get.

Winter

The winter season brings snow and ice to a lot of locations around the world. Most of the animals go in hibernation during this season, like bats, snakes, bears, skunks etc.

Autumn

This season is also known as the fall. It brings colder temperatures and frost. Some crops are harvested during autumn.

Spring

The spring season is a time of renewal. During this time, trees begin to develop (bud) and flowers start to bloom.
